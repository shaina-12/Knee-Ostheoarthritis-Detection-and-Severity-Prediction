About The Disease


Knee osteoarthritis (OA) is a degenerative knee
joint condition characterized by gradual wear and
strain and loss of articulatory cartilage. It
is typically found in adults. The common clinical
symptoms of knee osteoarthritis include knee pain that
gradually becomes worse over time along with knee stiffness
and swelling, pain after prolonged sitting or resting, and
pain that worsens over time. The severity of the symptoms
may differ from person to person. It affects more than 500
million people which constitutes about 7% of the
world's population with women disproportionately affected
by the condition. From 1990 to 2019, there was a 48% rise
in the cases of osteoarthritis globally, and in 2019 it
accounted for 2% of all years lived with disability (YLDs)
worldwide, ranking as the 15th most common cause of YLDs.
